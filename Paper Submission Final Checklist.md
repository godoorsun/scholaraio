# Paper Submission Final Checklist

> Final pre-submission checklist for polishing academic papers.  
> Intended for use in Codex, Claude Code, or manual proofreading workflows.

---

## 1. Writing Style and Expression

- [ ] Avoid vague expressions such as “related xxx”, “some aspects”, “various problems”, or other overly generic wording.
- [ ] Avoid informal self-promotional expressions such as “we proposed a new method”; prefer academically appropriate formulations such as “we present a novel …”, “we introduce a novel …”, or “this work proposes …”.
- [ ] Do not begin a sentence with “And”.
- [ ] Avoid using “for xxx” when a more precise construction such as “Regarding xxx” is more appropriate.
- [ ] Ensure each paragraph has a clear central idea and does not mix multiple unrelated points.
- [ ] Make sure each section title is specific to the actual problem addressed in the paper, rather than being so generic that it could fit any paper.
- [ ] Use precise terminology throughout the paper instead of broad or underspecified descriptions.

---

## 2. Related Work Writing

- [ ] Begin the related work section (or subsection) with 1–2 sentences that summarize the overall landscape of prior work.
- [ ] Present the relevant categories, lines of work, or representative methods in a clear and structured manner.
- [ ] After discussing the prior work, add a brief summary paragraph that synthesizes the above literature.
- [ ] Explicitly and briefly clarify how the current work differs from existing work and what gap it addresses.
- [ ] Keep the discussion focused and analytical rather than producing a disconnected list of papers.

---

## 3. Grammar and Sentence-Level Proofreading

- [ ] Check articles carefully (`a`, `an`, `the`).
- [ ] Check subject–verb agreement.
- [ ] Check tense consistency.
- [ ] Use past tense appropriately in related work and conclusions where needed.
- [ ] Check for run-on sentences.
- [ ] Check for sentence fragments.
- [ ] Make sure long sentences are readable and logically segmented.
- [ ] Ensure punctuation is complete and grammatically correct.

---

## 4. Cohesion, Logic, and Readability

- [ ] Check whether sentences within each paragraph connect naturally.
- [ ] Check whether paragraphs “hang together” logically.
- [ ] Make sure each paragraph begins with familiar/contextual information and ends with more informative or consequential content when possible.
- [ ] Ensure the topic of each paragraph can be identified quickly by the reader.
- [ ] Check for parallel structure in comparable expressions, lists, and contributions.
- [ ] Check normalization and wording uniformity across the manuscript.
- [ ] Make sure the flow between sections, paragraphs, and sentences is smooth and coherent.

---

## 5. Consistency

- [ ] Use the same spelling for the same term, dataset, method, system, author name, and acronym throughout the paper.
- [ ] Check that headings are capitalized consistently.
- [ ] Check that bulleted lists are capitalized consistently.
- [ ] Check that figure captions and table titles are capitalized consistently.
- [ ] Check that bulleted lists are punctuated consistently.
- [ ] Check that figure captions and table titles are punctuated consistently.
- [ ] Ensure wording choices are unified throughout the paper.

---

## 6. LaTeX-Specific Checks

- [ ] Add a non-breaking space before citations when needed, such as `~\cite{liu2016cognitive}`.
- [ ] Do not forget the tilde `~` before `\cite{}` where line breaks would be inappropriate.
- [ ] Add line breaks after each sentence in the `.tex` source file when helpful for proofreading and source-level readability.
- [ ] Check LaTeX source formatting for consistency and maintainability.
- [ ] Verify that no citation, reference, or formatting command is malformed.

---

## 7. Figures, Tables, and Visual Presentation

- [ ] Check figure captions and table titles for consistency in spelling, style, and punctuation.
- [ ] Ensure figures and tables appear close to the text that discusses them.
- [ ] Ensure figure and table numbering is in the correct order.
- [ ] Confirm that no figure is referenced before its actual number appears.
- [ ] Avoid overly simple figures with too little information content.
- [ ] Merge overly simple figures into richer composite figures when appropriate.
- [ ] Remove unnecessary large blank areas in figures.
- [ ] Make sure figures communicate substantial information rather than serving as decoration.
- [ ] Ensure the video, if included, highlights the interactivity of the system clearly.

---

## 8. Aesthetic and Layout Issues

- [ ] Check that line breaks and page breaks occur at sensible locations.
- [ ] Avoid awkward page starts, such as a page beginning with a single trailing word from the previous paragraph.
- [ ] Check the overall visual balance of the manuscript.
- [ ] Make sure tables, figures, equations, and paragraphs are not separated in visually confusing ways.

---

## 9. References

- [ ] Check reference completeness.
- [ ] Ensure no page numbers are missing where required.
- [ ] Ensure conference names are complete and correctly written.
- [ ] Ensure journal volume/issue information is complete where required.
- [ ] Ensure publication years are present and correct.
- [ ] Check reference formatting consistency throughout the bibliography.
- [ ] Make sure repeated journal or conference names are spelled exactly the same way everywhere.
- [ ] Verify that all in-text citations appear in the reference list and vice versa.

---

## 10. Final High-Level Review

- [ ] Every section has a clear purpose.
- [ ] Every paragraph has a clear topic.
- [ ] Every figure and table contributes meaningful evidence or explanation.
- [ ] The paper’s contribution is stated clearly and specifically.
- [ ] The distinction from prior work is explicit and convincing.
- [ ] The terminology is precise, consistent, and academically appropriate.
- [ ] The overall narrative is coherent from problem motivation to method, results, and conclusion.
- [ ] The manuscript is polished not only grammatically, but also logically, visually, and rhetorically.

---

## 11. Final Manual Pre-Submission Pass

- [ ] Recheck grammar, consistency, figures, tables, references, and layout one by one.
- [ ] Perform one last slow read focusing only on clarity, cohesion, and presentation quality.
- [ ] Confirm that nothing remains overly generic, under-explained, or visually weak.
- [ ] Confirm that the submission version is the correct and final version.
